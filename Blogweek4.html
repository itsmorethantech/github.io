How to build a PC. Part 3 

You have now gotten your PC! But you can’t use it yet!! 

You need to add the OS.

First, make sure you have access to your desired OS (I recommend Windows 10 have but you can also use Linux) 

And make sure you have downloaded it onto a USB drive or written it onto a disk (If you have a built-in or USB disk-reader). 

Be careful when downloading your OS onto a USB from a Mac because it formats USB drives differently from other systems. 

 

Next, you need to open your UEFI (Or BIOS). When you power on your PC, tap either the F1, F2, Fn + F2, Del, F12... it gets a tad complicated with all the differences between makers/manufacturers, so you should hold them all down or play around until something happens. 

When you get into it, you can then choose your boot device (USB or disk drive with the operating system), there are some other settings that you can change, but I recommend doing that part later. 

 

Setting up the OS is variable with so many different ones you might have, but it usually goes something like this: 

  1. Possibly agree to Terms & Conditions. 
  1. Connect to a network. 
  1. Set language if you have not chosen it during the download phase. 
  1. Set your date & time. 
  1. Maybe log in to your account. 
  1. Set up additional services like Cortana. 
  1. Then download the drivers for your PC parts. 

 

Then you are free to explore your new PC.
