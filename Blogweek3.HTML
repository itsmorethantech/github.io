How to build a computer. Part 2

Okay, the parts have arrived! A big bundle of strange bits of metal, plastic and cardboard packaging…

You are completely bewildered on what to do now!

Don’t worry, take a deep breath, Step 1 is to read the instructions.

Every part is different between manufacturers and models so make sure you read them. I also recommend, especially if you have carpets or like patting your pets, to use an anti-static band, these stop you from accidentally short circuiting your parts.

After that you need to put the CPU into the motherboard, this can be fiddly but make sure you line up the 2 triangles and don’t force it. Most CPUs already have thermal paste (to maximise contact with the fans) but you might need to put some on the top.

After putting the cpu on the motherboard you can add the fans, remember to not force anything!

The next step is to screw in the motherboard to your case, this can be a little tricky because of how many screws there are but line it up so the most amount of the holes in the motherboard is lined up with the holes in the case.

After that you should probably slot in the RAM and screw in the hard drive.

This part is mostly easy.

Then you can slot in the graphic card (which is a little complicated because of how big the manufacturers’ definition of a card is)

And then it’s just wiring, make sure you connect everything properly.

And there is your PC!

Check next week for how to do the software!
