How to build a PC. Part 1

Maybe you want to save money? Maybe you want custom colours? Or maybe you just want a challenge? Then this is the guide for you…

PC’s are made up of a variety of different parts, from RAM to GPU to CPU to Motherboards to Storage. You get to decide which parts and what type you want.

A motherboard is the part which holds all the other parts together, It connects everything, It is like the veins of your computer.

One of the other important parts is the CPU, Central-Processing-Unit. This is where everything is processed. It is responsible for taking the data from other hardware and software and “outputting”, sending out, the data which is needed. The CPU is a bit like the brains of your computer.

Then comes the confusing parts, memory (RAM) and storage (hard drive)…

You might be like “What’s the difference? They both just store data!”, but they are actually both used for different jobs! The storage is like our long term memory, it is much bigger than our short term memory but it is waaayy slower to access…

This is where your files are stored most of the time.

“If everything is stored in the storage, what about the memory though?” The memory is used for storing the files that you are viewing right now.

It is super quick but takes up much more physical space per amount of data it can store. It is like your short term memory.

If you are playing, for example, Minecraft, The memory in your computer is storing the textures of the blocks that you are rendering and part of the world file corresponding to the area you are in.

The other parts like sound-card and graphics-card (GPU) are pretty self explanatory, if you are using your computer with lots of music (for example if you are a songwriter or like making electronic songs) you can get a sound-card but in lots of mother boards there is built in sound and same sort of thing with the GPU (I recommend getting one if you like gaming or are a graphic designer).
